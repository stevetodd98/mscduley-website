Who We Are

The Fleet Reserve Association (FRA) is a congressionally chartered, non-profit organization that represents the interests of the Sea Service community before the U.S. Congress. Since 1924, the FRA has been the premier voice for enlisted personnel of the **U.S. Navy, Marine Corps, and Coast Guard**.

"With reverence for God and country, and being ever mindful of the glorious traditions of the United States Navy, Marine Corps, and Coast Guard... we associate ourselves together."

Our Mission

The FRA's primary mission is to protect the pay, benefits, and compensation of Sea Services personnel—active duty, reserve, retired, and veteran—and their families through direct contact with elected officials.

History of Advocacy

Founded by Navy Chief Yeoman George L. Carlin and chartered on November 11, 1924, the FRA was the first organization to exclusively represent the interests of enlisted personnel before Congress. Today, we continue that legacy by maintaining close liaison with the Departments of Defense, Veterans Affairs, and Homeland Security.

A Community of Shipmates

Beyond the halls of Washington D.C., the FRA is a community. We believe that a "Shipmate is a Shipmate," regardless of rank or background. Through our local branches, like Branch 172 in Yorktown, we provide a homeport for fellowship, support in times of distress, and a shared dedication to the traditions of the sea.
