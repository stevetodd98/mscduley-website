100 Years of Advocacy

The Fleet Reserve Association (FRA) is the premier voice for enlisted sea service members on Capitol Hill. Founded in 1924, we were the first organization to exclusively represent the needs of the enlisted ranks of the Navy, Marine Corps, and Coast Guard.

Who We Represent

The FRA represents the interests of Sea Service personnel across all phases of their careers:

  • Active Duty & Reserve: Advocating for fair pay, housing allowances, and deployment protections.
  • Retirees: Protecting earned pension benefits and COLA increases.
  • Veterans & Families: Ensuring access to quality VA health care and TRICARE stability.

Centennial Milestones

1924: Founded by Navy Chief Yeoman George L. Carlin to bridge the gap between enlisted personnel and Congress.
1946: Instrumental in passing the Armed Forces Leave Act, granting enlisted members the same leave rights as officers.
1984: Successfully lobbied for the Montgomery GI Bill, transforming educational opportunities for veterans.
2024: Celebrated 100 years of continuous service and advocacy in Washington, D.C.
Today: Branch 172 continues this legacy right here in Yorktown, supporting our local shipmates.

The Branch 172 Legacy

In Yorktown, we provide a "homeport" for these national missions. We meet monthly to discuss legislative updates, support our local shipmates, and honor the traditions that define our services. At the FRA, we believe that a Shipmate is a Shipmate—no matter the branch or the era of service.
