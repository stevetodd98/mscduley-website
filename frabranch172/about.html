100 Years of Advocacy

The Fleet Reserve Association (FRA) is more than just a veterans' group—it is the direct link between the enlisted sea services and the lawmakers in Washington, D.C. Founded in 1924, the FRA was the first organization to represent the specific needs of the enlisted ranks of the Navy, Marine Corps, and Coast Guard.

Who We Represent

The FRA represents the interests of Sea Service personnel across all phases of their careers:

  • Active Duty: Fighting for fair pay and housing allowances.
  • Reservists: Ensuring deployment protections and benefit parity.
  • Retirees: Protecting COLA increases and TRICARE access.
  • Veterans: Advocacy for VA health care and disability recognition.

The Branch 172 Legacy

Here in Yorktown, Branch 172 provides the "homeport" for these national missions. We meet monthly to discuss legislative updates, support our local shipmates, and honor the traditions that define the Sea Services. We believe that a Shipmate is a Shipmate—no matter the rank, no matter the branch.
