A Legacy of Sea Power

Established on October 13, 1775, the United States Navy was born out of the necessity to defend the colonies from the British fleet. From the wooden hulls of the original six frigates to the nuclear-powered supercarriers of today, the Navy remains the world's premier maritime force.

⚓ The "Oldest" Commissioned Ship
The USS Constitution ("Old Ironsides"), launched in 1797, is still a commissioned U.S. Navy vessel. She earned her nickname during the War of 1812 when British cannonballs were seen bouncing off her resilient live oak hull.
⚓ The Six Original Frigates
In 1794, Congress authorized the construction of six frigates to combat Barbary pirates. One of these, the USS United States, was the first ship to ever fly the "Stars and Stripes" in a naval battle.
⚓ Breaking the Sound Barrier at Sea
The U.S. Navy didn't just master the water; it mastered the sky. Navy Captain Chuck Yeager may have been Air Force, but Navy Commander Alan Shepard was the first American in space, and Navy aviator Neil Armstrong was the first man on the moon.
⚓ "I Have Not Yet Begun to Fight!"
During the Battle of Flamborough Head in 1779, John Paul Jones uttered these famous words when asked to surrender his sinking ship. He went on to capture the British ship Serapis, securing a legendary victory.
