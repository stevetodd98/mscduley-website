Shield of the Freedom

The Coast Guard is one of the nation's five armed services, unique in its dual role as a military service and a federal law enforcement agency. Founded by Alexander Hamilton as the **Revenue Marine**, it is the oldest continuous seagoing service of the United States.

‚öì The "Revenue Cutter Service"
For several years after the Continental Navy was disbanded, the Revenue Marine (later the Coast Guard) was the nation's *only* armed force afloat. They were responsible for protecting the coast from smugglers and ensuring the collection of vital customs duties.
‚öì The Racing Stripe
The iconic red and blue "Racing Stripe" seen on Coast Guard vessels was adopted in 1967. It was designed to give Coast Guard assets a distinctive look that would be easily recognized from a distance, even by international vessels.
‚öì Douglas Munro: A Hero of Guadalcanal
Signalman 1st Class Douglas Munro is the only Coast Guardsman to receive the Medal of Honor. He was posthumously awarded for his heroic actions in evacuating a detachment of Marines under heavy fire at Point Cruz, Guadalcanal, in 1942.
‚öì Lifesavers from the Start
In 1915, the Revenue Cutter Service merged with the U.S. Life-Saving Service to form the modern Coast Guard. Today, they perform an average of 45 search and rescue cases every single day.
