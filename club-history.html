Horace Ray Duley (1919-2021)

First President and Chairman of the Board

Born on March 22, 1919, in Mt. Vernon, Indiana, Mr. Duley grew up on a farm along the Ohio River. Watching the steamboats pass his childhood home sparked a lifelong love of the water that led him to a distinguished 23-year career in the U.S. Navy.

⚓ Senior Chief Gunner’s Mate (GMCS) | USN 1939–1962

The "Writing on the Wall": In October 1939, as war erupted in Europe, Mr. Duley enlisted in the Navy. His service spanned the entirety of WWII, the Korean War, and the early Cold War, retiring in June 1962 at the senior enlisted rank of GMCS.

The Doolittle Raid & Midway: Serving aboard the heavy cruiser USS Vincennes (CA-44), he witnessed the launch of the B-25 bombers for the Doolittle Raid and defended the fleet during the pivotal Battle of Midway.

The "Water Geyser" Defense: In August 1942, during the invasion of Guadalcanal, Mr. Duley utilized his expertise as a Gunner’s Mate to defend the *Vincennes* against over 30 Japanese warplanes. He and his crew used the 8-inch guns to fire into the ocean in front of low-flying planes, creating massive geysers that tore the aircraft apart as they plowed into the water.

Savo Island Survival: After surviving the sinking of the *Vincennes* on August 9, 1942, he continued his Pacific service aboard the USS Santa Fe (CL-60). Following his Naval retirement, he served as a civilian at the Naval Weapons Station Yorktown until 1976.

The Charter Seven: 1977 Founding Directors

Alongside Mr. Duley, six other Shipmates signed the original 1977 Articles of Incorporation, dedicating themselves to the "Protection, Loyalty, and Service" of enlisted veterans.

Gordon H. Webster

Yeoman Second Class (YN2) | USN (Ret)

Administrative Architect: Born in New Haven, CT, Gordon served from July 1948 through March 1971. As a Yeoman, his administrative precision was vital to the club’s founding. Serving as the first Vice President, he was the primary leader alongside Mr. Duley who navigated the complex legal process of securing our original charter and property.

Service Period: 07/1948 – 03/1971 | Birthplace: New Haven, CT

<

Blackledge "Blackie" E. Harper

Chief Torpedoman’s Mate, Submarines (TMC (SS)) | USN (Ret)

A Legacy of the Silent Service: Blackie served from February 1945 through October 1966, a career spanning WWII, Korea, and the Cold War. A qualified submariner, his service took him from the destroyer USS John W. Weeks to the deep-sea missions of the submarine USS Chivo (SS-341).

Duty Stations: USS John W. Weeks (DD-701), USS Salinan (ATF-161), USS Chivo (SS-341), USS Fulton (AS-11), and Fleet Training Center Newport.

Harry C. Depue, Jr.

Electrician’s Mate First Class (EM1) | USN (Ret)

Technical Leadership: Born in Spencer, WV, Harry served a dedicated 20-year career from March 1940 through December 1960. As an Electrician’s Mate, he brought vital technical expertise to the board. He was instrumental during the club’s early years, focusing on the infrastructure and physical maintenance of the original Cook Road facility.

Service Period: 03/1940 – 12/1960 | Birthplace: Spencer, WV

Gary C. Thompson

Boatswain’s Mate First Class (BM1) | USN (Ret)

Organizational Leadership: Born in Willis Branch, WV, Gary served from February 1947 through December 1966. As a Boatswain’s Mate, he brought the discipline and traditional leadership of the Navy’s oldest rating to the club. He was a cornerstone of the board during the pivotal transition and expansion of the club's charter in 1979.

Service Period: 02/1947 – 12/1966 | Birthplace: Willis Branch, WV

Curtis E. Smith

Boatswain’s Mate First Class (BM1) | USN (Ret)

A Master of Seamanship: Born in Petersburg, VA, Curtis enlisted in February 1943 during the height of WWII and served a distinguished career through July 1962. As a Boatswain’s Mate, he represented the traditional heart of the Navy. He was a vital link between the club and the local military community during the 1977 founding, embodying the principle of Loyalty to the Sea Services.

Service Period: 02/1943 – 07/1962 | Birthplace: Petersburg, VA

George E. Kelley

Navy Veteran | Director

Bylaw & Social Architect: George was a dedicated resident of the Peninsula who answered the call to lead the club during its 1977 incorporation. He played a crucial role in drafting the original organizational structure, ensuring the club remained a "non-profit and non-stock" corporation dedicated solely to the welfare of its members. His commitment to the club’s social mission helped create the welcoming atmosphere that defines the Social Quarter today.

Eternal Rest: October 27, 2008 | Hampton, VA

Corporate Milestones

September 23, 1977: Incorporated as FRA Branch 172 Home, Inc. by the original Charter Seven.
September 10, 1979: Articles amended to Fleet Reserve Association Branch 172 Home, Inc. and the board expanded to nine members.
September 27, 2021: Formally renamed to Military Services Club Duley to permanently honor our first Chairman.
