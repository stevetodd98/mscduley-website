Horace Ray Duley (1919-2021)

First Chairman of the Board & Club Namesake

A native of Portsmouth and a 22.5-year veteran of the U.S. Navy, Horace Ray Duley witnessed the most pivotal moments of the 20th Century. His leadership laid the foundation for the Military Services Club we enjoy today.

⚓ A World War II Hero

During his Naval service, Mr. Duley participated in the Doolittle Raid and the Battle of Midway. He survived the Battle of Savo Island and was present in Tokyo Bay aboard the USS Missouri to witness the formal surrender of Japan, ending World War II.

The Original Charter

The organization was officially incorporated on September 23, 1977, as a non-profit corporation in Yorktown, Virginia. Originally founded as FRA Branch 172 Home, Inc., the mission was clear: to provide assistance to shipmates in need and a home for those who served.

1977: Incorporation
Founded by seven original directors at the initial registered office on Cook Road.
1979: Expansion & Name Change
On September 10, 1979, the board expanded to nine directors and officially changed the name to Fleet Reserve Association Branch 172 Home, Inc.

The Founding Directors (The "Charter Seven")

We honor the seven individuals who signed the original articles of incorporation to create a sanctuary for Sea Service veterans in Yorktown:

Horace R. Duley
Gordon H. Webster
Blackledge E. Harper
Gary C. Thompson
George E. Kelley
Curtis E. Smith
Harry C. Depue, Jr.

A Living Tradition

Today, the Military Services Club Duley continues to honor the "Cardinal Principles" established by these founders. We remain a member-driven venue where fellowship, advocacy, and community engagement thrive for veterans and their families.
