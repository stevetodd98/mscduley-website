Horace Ray Duley (1919-2021)

Founder, First President, and Chairman of the Board

A native of Portsmouth and a 22.5-year veteran of the U.S. Navy, Mr. Duley was the architect of our community. He lived to the age of 102, leaving behind a legacy that defines the Military Services Club today.

⚓ World War II Naval Record

During his distinguished career, Mr. Duley witnessed history firsthand. He was part of the task force for the Doolittle Raid, fought in the Battle of Midway, and survived the Battle of Savo Island. He came full circle in his service by witnessing the formal Japanese surrender aboard the USS Missouri in Tokyo Bay on September 2, 1945.

The Charter Seven: 1977 Founding Directors

Alongside Mr. Duley, six other Shipmates signed the original 1977 Articles of Incorporation, dedicating themselves to the "Protection, Loyalty, and Service" of enlisted veterans.

Gordon H. Webster

Senior Enlisted | Vice President

The primary operational leader alongside Mr. Duley who helped secure the initial property and incorporation status on Cook Road.

Blackledge "Blackie" Harper

Senior Chief Petty Officer | Director

A WWII veteran and career sailor known for his steadfast dedication to the FRA mission and the security of the club's home.

Harry C. Depue, Jr.

Chief Petty Officer | Director

A veteran of the Korean War era who was instrumental in the physical maintenance and early growth of the Branch.

George E. Kelley

Navy Veteran | Director

A Yorktown resident and original board member who helped draft the bylaws protecting the club's non-profit status.

Gary C. Thompson

Navy Veteran | Director

Provided vital organizational leadership during the transition and expansion of the Board of Directors in 1979.

Curtis E. Smith

Navy Veteran | Director

Represented the core value of Loyalty, acting as a liaison between the Branch and local military commands.

Corporate Milestones

September 23, 1977: Incorporated as FRA Branch 172 Home, Inc. by the original Charter Seven.
September 10, 1979: Articles amended to Fleet Reserve Association Branch 172 Home, Inc. and the board expanded to nine members.
September 27, 2021: Formally renamed to Military Services Club Duley to permanently honor our first Chairman.
