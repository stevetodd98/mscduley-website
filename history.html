June in Military History

Honoring the defining moments, turning points, and branch birthdays celebrated across our Joint Forces this month.

June 3-7, 1942: The Battle of Midway

Widely regarded as the turning point of the war in the Pacific. Thanks to brilliant codebreaking and the unmatched courage of Navy aviators, American carrier forces dealt a decisive blow to the Imperial Japanese Navy, sinking four of their frontline aircraft carriers and altering the course of World War II.

June 6, 1944: D-Day (Operation Overlord)

Marking its 82nd Anniversary, the largest amphibious invasion in world history commenced as Allied forces stormed the fortified beaches of Normandy, France. Securing a bloody but crucial foothold, the bravery of these soldiers, sailors, and airmen began the liberation of Western Europe from Nazi control.

June 14, 1775: U.S. Army Birthday & Flag Day

The U.S. Army celebrates 251 years of dedicated service to the nation. Established by the Continental Congress in 1775 under General George Washington, the Army stands as America's oldest military branch. Coinciding with Flag Day, it highlights the enduring defense of our national colors.

June 17, 1898: Navy Hospital Corps Founded

President William McKinley signed the bill establishing the U.S. Navy Hospital Corps. From World War battlefields to modern operations, Corpsmen have served alongside sailors and Marines, earning more citations for gallantry than any other corps in the Navy.

June 22, 1944: The G.I. Bill is Signed

President Franklin D. Roosevelt signed the historic Servicemen's Readjustment Act. This landmark legislation completely transformed American society, providing returning World War II veterans with critical access to higher education, home loans, and unemployment insurance.

June 23, 1939: U.S. Coast Guard Auxiliary Birthday

Originally created as the Coast Guard Reserve and later redesignated as the Auxiliary, this vital volunteer force supports recreational boating safety and maritime security, augmenting active-duty missions across our coastal waterways.

June 25, 1950: The Korean War Begins

North Korean forces launched a massive surprise attack across the 38th Parallel, invading South Korea. This aggression prompted immediate United Nations intervention, drawing American forces into a bitter, three-year conflict to stop the spread of communism.
